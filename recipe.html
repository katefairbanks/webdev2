Overview

Quick and delicious, this recipe is easily one of my favorites. I can pull things out of the freezer and quickly put it together. The longest step in this recipe is waiting for the water to boil!

This recipe is so fun to add different things to and make it your own. In the recipe I listed some popular add-ins, but don’t feel limited to what I’ve listed, if you have something else you want to add in, go for it. Get creative!

Lots of kids don’t like broccoli, and there's a simple way around that - let them help make it! They’ll be so excited that they made dinner that they will forget that they don’t like broccoli.




How do I know if my pasta is done?

If for some reason your tortellini doesn't tell you how long to cook it for, an easy well to tell if it's done is once all the pasta floats up to the top. I usually like to let it sit wiht them all floating for an extra minute just to make sure all of them are done. Be carefull though that you don't wait too long becasue you don't want the toretellini to overcook and get all mushy. Tortellini is best al dante, which means tender, but still firm.

You can also prick a tortellini with a fork or toothpick to check if the middle is done. When you do this the fork should slide in easily. Be careful when using this method, becuase a mushy tortellini will also be easy to stab with a fork. Keep an eye on your pasta as it cooks quicky and if you're not careful you will likely overcook it whcih will ruin the texture of the meal.

How to Make Handmade Tortellini - BROOKLYN PASTA LAB







Recipe

Cook Time: 10min

Servings: 4

Ingredients

    - One 12- or 16-ounce bag frozen cheese tortellini
    - One 16-ounce bag frozen broccoli florets, or 1 head broccoli, cut into small florets (5 to 6 cups)
    - 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
    - ¼ cup grated parmesan cheese
    - ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
    - ⅛ teaspoon garlic powder (100% a suggestion, you can never have too much garlic so put as much as you want in it)

Instructions

  1. In a medium or large saucepan, bring water to a boil and dump both the bag of frozen tortellini and frozen broccoli florets in to cook according to the tortellini instructions (usually about 6 minutes). If your tortellini takes longer, wait to put broccoli in until 5 minutes before tortellini is done. Broccoli and tortellini should be tender.

  2. Drain and return to the pan.

  3. Immediately add the parmesan cheese, oil, salt and garlic and any other desired add-ins. Stir well to combine. Popular add-ins are:
      4. - Walnuts
      - Lemon juice (fresh squeezed is best)
      - Fresh cracked pepper
      - Spinach (can add in alongside the broccoli)
      - Cranberries
      - Pesto
      - Chicken
      - Bits of fresh bacon
      - Shrimp
      - Sliced cherry tomatoes

  4. Serve while warm.

  5. If there are leftovers, refrigerate for up to 3 days. After reheating add a little more oil in to freshen it up.
